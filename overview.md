### Sourcing 

Our project examines the factors that influence happiness levels across countries using data from the World Happiness Report, 2020. Our main goal is to understand how different variables such as income, social support, life expectancy, freedom, generosity, and perceptions of corrpution relate to a country's overall happiness score. 

To support our analysis, we used the World Happiness Report dataset as our primary source. This dataset includes happiness scores and socioeconomic indicators for countries around the world. We also selected scholarly articles and credible reports that discuss how happiness is measured and why happiness levels differ across populations. These sources provide important background for interpreting the patterns shown in our data visualizations. 

Our secondary sources also help explain why economic wealth alone does not always determine happiness.
